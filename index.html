Anjaly Kalayil

I am an animator exploring humor through character-driven storytelling and sound. My latest project features a cleaning robot who, in his passion for singing, neglects his chores and stumbles into trouble.

Spinning Into Trouble

This short film follows a spirited little cleaning robot with a passion for music and an unfortunate knack for chaos. Tasked with tidying up, the robots enthusiasm quickly shifts to an impromptu musical performance. As it gets lost in song, the robot's playful personality unfolds, and its clumsiness causes hilarious mishaps around the room. Through a blend of comedic timing, physical comedy, and an expressive soundtrack, the film explores the idea that sometimes, our passions lead us astray but reveal our truest selves. I am exploring the potential of sound and music as narrative tools, specifically in the context of character-driven comedy. The concept or inquiry behind this work is how sound and music can replace traditional forms of dialogue while still conveying humor, emotion, and story progression. The goal is to investigate how music can become more than just background noise, instead serving as the main driving force of the narrative. One key theme in this story is the joy of self-expression, represented by the robots uncontainable urge to sing and celebrate, even at the cost of its main responsibility.

Meniscus

Meniscus is a linear 3D animation that uses hand gestures as an input method and a cognitive tool to navigate through its surreal and abstract narrative. Viewers can interact with the animation through the representation and combination of three distinct gestures: Open palm, Closed fist and Pointing up. The animation follows a character within his dream and viewers must use specific hand gestures to interact with and guide the character and his actions, helping him wake up. This interaction between viewers and the animation can evoke a shifting sense of control throughout the narrative and hints at our own struggles with electronic devices and digital media. We percieve his dream world not only through words and images but also through our bodily experience, helping us understand the narrative by embodying the characters journey and empathising with his challenges. Gesture based interaction is intuitive and organic, requiring little to no briefing for viewers to engage with the animatic. By incorporating these interactions, the projects aims to deepen the connection and empathy between viewers and the narrative, inviting them to actively participate rather than be passive observers.

A highly sensitive experience

A Highly Sensitive Experience is a first-person virtual reality interactive developed using Unity and designed for the Meta Quest 2 focusing on sensory overload and potential coping techniques. The player engages in the experience through an immersive first-person perspective, using the VR headset and standard controllers. By interacting with various environmental stimuli, the player encounters sensory overload from the perspective of a highly sensitive person (HSP). Set in a dark, sensory-rich environment, with carefully designed soundscapes and embodied interactions, the experience simulates the overwhelming sensations often felt by HSPs. Players are invited to engage with actions such as wearing earphones, turning off mobile notification and taking shower to navigate through the environment which reference coping techniques. This project seeks to foster greater understanding of the challenges faced by highly sensitive individuals and to offer coping strategies to those who identify with the condition.
